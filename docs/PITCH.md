# The Flock Pitch — DLBC Leadership

## Who to Talk To, In What Order

1. **Your dad (Group Coordinator)** — pilot within his group first. No permission needed, no bureaucracy. 3-5 branches, 2-3 months.
2. **Benue Central State Overseer** — you have the existing ASKTC relationship. Come with pilot data, not just a pitch.
3. **State Administration Team** — the Overseer will introduce you. This is where formal rollout happens.
4. **National HQ** — only approach after 2+ states are live. You're not asking for permission at that point.

---

## The Pitch (State Overseer Version)

> "Sir, you currently know what happened in a branch in February when it's already April. By the time that attendance record reaches you, it's passed through four people's hands and lost all the qualitative context — the testimonies, the prayer requests, the things that make the numbers mean something.
>
> Flock gives you visibility in real time. A branch secretary records Sunday's attendance on Sunday evening — on her phone, even without internet. By Monday morning, you can see every submission across the state, flag any anomalies, and the month-end report compiles itself.
>
> We're already piloting this within [Dad's] group. [Dad] used to spend hours at month end compiling reports from WhatsApp messages. Now it takes minutes.
>
> I'm not asking for anything today except the opportunity to extend this to two or three more groups in Benue State. If it serves the church well, we can talk about a broader rollout."

---

## Framing Principles

- This is a **ministry tool**, not a software product
- Lead with **stewardship** — accountability for offerings and attendance is a spiritual responsibility
- Lead with **burden reduction** — secretaries and admins are volunteers; you're giving them time back
- Lead with **visibility** — not efficiency metrics, but the ability to serve people better when you can see what's happening
- Never use startup language (runway, ARR, churn, pivot)

---

## Objections and Responses

**"We already have a system / WhatsApp works fine"**
> "WhatsApp shows you what someone decided to send you. Flock shows you what's actually happening. The difference matters when a branch's attendance drops 30% and nobody thinks to mention it."

**"What happens to our data?"**
> "Your data is yours. It's stored on secure servers and never shared with anyone outside DLBC. If you ever decide to stop using Flock, you get a full export."

**"Is this free?"**
> "The pilot is completely free. We'll talk about a modest monthly contribution only after you've confirmed it's genuinely useful."

**"Who else is using this?"**
> (During pilot phase) "We're starting with DLBC Benue because of our existing relationship with your ministry. You'll be the first denomination we serve — which means you get to shape what it looks like."
